Foods in Baguio City

Strawberry Taho

Strawberry taho is one of Baguio’s most iconic street foods, offering a delicious twist on the classic Filipino snack. Instead of traditional caramel syrup, Baguio’s version uses a rich, tangy strawberry sauce made from freshly harvested strawberries. This gives the taho a balance of sweetness and tartness, complementing the soft silken tofu and chewy sago pearls. Vendors roam around popular spots like Burnham Park, calling out "Taho!" as they serve this warm and comforting treat.

Strawberry Shortcake

Strawberry shortcake is a must-try dessert in Baguio, featuring soft sponge cake layered with whipped cream and freshly sliced strawberries. Thanks to Baguio’s cool climate, the strawberries are naturally sweet and juicy. Among the bakeries offering this delightful treat, Vizco’s is the most famous, drawing long lines of visitors eager to get a taste. Many tourists buy a slice or even a whole cake to take home as a souvenir.

Ube Jam

Ube jam is a popular pasalubong (souvenir treat) from Baguio, known for its rich, creamy texture and sweet, earthy flavor. Made from purple yam, sugar, and milk, it is slow-cooked to perfection. Good Shepherd Convent is the most famous producer, with sales supporting scholarships for underprivileged students. Ube jam is delicious on bread, as a topping for desserts, or even as an ingredient in ice cream and pastries.

Pinikpikan

Pinikpikan is a traditional Cordilleran dish deeply rooted in indigenous culture. This savory chicken soup is prepared using a unique method—before cooking, the chicken is lightly beaten, enhancing its flavor by keeping the blood inside the meat. The chicken is then roasted and boiled with native herbs and vegetables like sayote. Some variations include etag, a smoked and salted pork that adds a rich umami taste. Pinikpikan is a must-try for those looking to experience authentic local flavors.
